Lifting Rules

Superset Execution

  • Set up everything in the block before you start. Belt squat loaded, pull-up bar within reach, DBs in hand — eliminate the walk-around between exercises.
  • Within a round, transition fast (10–20 sec). Between rounds, take the full prescribed rest.
  • If a heavy main lift (belt squat, hex dead) starts grinding by round 3, take 30 extra sec between rounds. Don't compromise the anchor lift to hit the clock.
  • Accessory partner in a heavy block (pull-ups, lateral raises) is the active recovery — never push to failure during the heavy block. Save the redline for Block D.
  • If equipment competes, default to circuit format: all sets of A1, then all sets of A2, etc. You'll lose ~5 min.

Progression

  • Hex bar deadlift: starting at 225 (re-acclimation). Add 20 lbs per session at RPE ≤7 until ~315, then taper to +10 lbs.
  • Belt squat: pulley upgrade done (doubles effective load). Progress weight normally; when a set feels easy, add 1–2 reps or tempo (3-sec → 4-sec descent) before bumping load.
  • Bench: add 5 lbs when 4×8 moves at RPE 7 or lower.
  • Accessories: add 1–2 reps before adding weight. At the top of the rep range across all sets, bump the load.
  • RPE 9+ on the last set of a main lift → hold weight one more session.
  • Low-recovery day (Whoop yellow) → drop one round from each block, keep the weight.
  • On the main lifts (dead, belt squat) you CAN chase RPE 8–9. On the paired accessory in Block A, leave 3–4 in the tank — it's active recovery.

Recovery & Walking

Daily Walking

Target 10,000+ steps. Non-negotiable minimum: 8,000. Morning walk with Badger ~30–45 min (3–4.5k steps), afternoon/evening walk 45–60 min (4.5–6k steps).

4 PM check: under 6k steps = evening walk is mandatory.

Decision Framework

  • FTP/MAP improve AND body comp improves → protocol works, continue.
  • FTP/MAP hold AND body comp improves → reduced cardio is sufficient, continue.
  • FTP/MAP decline AND body comp improves → add 3rd Norway next block (Fri becomes default, golf-flex shifts to Thu PM).
  • Strength plateaus but body comp improves → check protein intake first, then consider a 4th lift per week.
  • Everything declines → return to structured SYSTM plan, home gym as supplement only.
